# 🎲 Gacha Simulator: A Behavioral Awareness Tool for Digital Risk Exposure

## Overview

This project presents a lightweight simulation tool designed to illustrate the behavioral and psychological mechanisms underlying **gacha-based monetization systems** in digital games.

🔗 Live Demo:  
https://anggaconni.github.io/gatcha-sim/

The simulator aims to support **digital literacy, child protection, and behavioral awareness efforts** by translating abstract probabilistic systems into observable user experiences.

---

## Background

Gacha systems are widely implemented in free-to-play digital games, where users spend virtual or real currency to obtain randomized rewards (e.g., characters, skins, items).

Unlike direct purchases, gacha mechanics rely on **probability-based outcomes**, often with very low success rates for high-value items.

These systems are structurally comparable to mechanisms studied in behavioral psychology, particularly:

- Variable ratio reinforcement schedules  
- Near-miss effects  
- Reward anticipation loops  

Such mechanisms are recognized for their potential to **increase engagement and repetitive behavior**, especially among vulnerable populations.

---

## Problem Statement

Field observations and anecdotal evidence indicate that:

- Children are increasingly exposed to gacha systems through mobile games  
- High-value digital items are not directly purchasable, requiring repeated randomized attempts  
- In some cases, significant real-world spending occurs without clear awareness of cumulative cost  

This raises concerns regarding:

- Early exposure to gambling-like systems  
- Financial literacy gaps among minors  
- Behavioral conditioning toward risk-based reward systems  

---

## Objective

This simulator was developed to:

1. Provide a **transparent representation** of gacha mechanics  
2. Demonstrate the **probabilistic nature of rewards**  
3. Visualize **cumulative financial implications**  
4. Encourage **critical reflection on user behavior**  

---

## Features

- **Transparent Probability Display**  
  (e.g., SSR rate = 1%)

- **Real-World Cost Simulation**  
  Each pull corresponds to a monetary value

- **User Behavior Tracking**
  - Total number of pulls  
  - Estimated spending  
  - Rare item acquisition  

- **Embedded Behavioral Insights**
  - Reinforcement patterns  
  - Near-miss scenarios

## Relevance to Policy and Practice

This tool may be relevant for:

- Digital literacy programs  
- Child protection initiatives  
- Behavioral research and public awareness campaigns  
- Stakeholder engagement (educators, parents, policymakers)  

Several jurisdictions have begun regulating or restricting certain implementations of randomized monetization systems due to their potential impact on minors.

---

## Methodology

The simulator models a simplified gacha system using:

- Fixed probability distribution  
- Repeated randomized trials  
- Accumulated cost representation  

The design prioritizes **clarity and transparency over gamification** to emphasize educational value.

---

## Limitations

- Does not replicate full commercial game dynamics  
- Behavioral responses may vary across users  
- Not intended as a predictive or diagnostic tool  

---

## Ethical Considerations

This project is developed strictly for:

- Educational purposes  
- Awareness building  

It does not promote or incentivize gambling or in-game spending.
